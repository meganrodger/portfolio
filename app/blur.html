The blur between performances of the self

MA SELF DIRECTED

A research project into the behavioural changes and constructs that happen when we become aware of being watched.

Through observing our behavioural habits with social media had become interested in how people performed certain aspects of their lives in front of a camera. Conducting research in the various ways that people do this, from catching the moment that people are aware that they are in a public space, to going as an extra on reality tv shows where cast members play versions of themselves on and off of the camera, I began to want to find a way to see if I could capture this behaviour in real life.

After looking at the promenade as a old way that people presented themselves in the public arena, I began a series of iterative experiments trying to record and compare the way people’s behaviour changes when they are aware of being filmed. This involved surreptitiously filming someone as they walked through a park, approaching them to ask if they would allow to re-record them to allow me to compare their gait and body language, and then re-walk the same stretch.

Collecting dual sets of footage each time, I became interested in them when I started overlaying the two and observing the blur between the two pieces of footage; an illustration of the change. Devising more accurate, and more pronounced ways of capturing this through each attempt,

Creating one last experiment to see how far I could manipulate the blur between the two moving footages, taking advantage of they consciousness of repeated actions and time in front of the camera, I conducted a workshop, with an elaborate construction to capture their movement before they arrived, Asking them to try capture aspects of their exaggerated performed personality in the workshop I had a series of characters overlayed with the unconscious version of that person.
